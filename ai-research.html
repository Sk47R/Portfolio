"Teaching machines to see the world"

AI & Computer Vision Research

2 peer-reviewed publications · 2 research roles

My research sits at the intersection of 3D vision and vision-language models, focusing on reconstructing real places in 3D, teaching models to describe what they see in those scenes, and benchmarking how well these systems perform. Both research roles below were supervised by Dr. Danda Pani Paudel .

3D Reconstruction of Heritage Structures

Undergraduate major project · Apr 2024 – May 2025

The modeling side of a larger heritage-preservation effort — building the dataset and training the 3D reconstruction models that the virtual tour (see Web Development) is built on.

  • Built custom image datasets through structured collection and preprocessing pipelines, including semantic segmentation and filtering for 3D reconstruction.
  • Trained and benchmarked multiple 3D Gaussian Splatting models such as Hierarchical GS, CityGaussian, and gsplat along with integration of bilateral grids and appearance embeddings to improve reconstruction quality.
  • Evaluated reconstruction quality across model variants to guide which approach the project standardized on.

Virtual Try-On of Shoes

Undergraduate minor project · Nov 2023 – Mar 2024

A real-time AR try-on system — from a small custom dataset to a live mobile pipeline that overlays a 3D shoe model onto a user's feet.

  • Collected and curated a 100+ image dataset, then fine-tuned YOLOv7 for instance segmentation across varied real-world conditions.
  • Applied 3D Gaussian Splatting and SuGaR mesh extraction to reconstruct accurate 3D shoe geometry from segmented images.
  • Deployed a real-time inference pipeline in Snapchat's Lens Studio, with live feet detection and 3D mesh overlay on mobile devices.

Dataset curation, model fine-tuning, AR deployment

SCOPE: Semantic Captioning for Optimized Photo Exploration

Applied Data Science capstone · Dec 2024

An image-captioning system designed for edge deployment that compares model architectures not only on accuracy but also on their robustness under compression.

  • Applied pruning, quantization, and knowledge distillation to transformer models for edge deployment, systematically analyzing the accuracy-efficiency trade-off.
  • Built a Streamlit interface for end-to-end model inference and evaluation.

Derma: Skin Disease Classification

Hackathon project

A deep learning model for multi-class skin disease classification, built during a hackathon — combining transfer learning with custom data collection and careful dataset balancing to improve real-world generalization.

  • Developed a multi-class skin disease classifier using a Kaggle dataset supplemented with ~300 self-collected images of clear skin.
  • Preprocessed and augmented images using OpenCV and TensorFlow/Keras, applying contrast enhancement, resizing, normalization, rotation, flipping, and brightness adjustments to improve generalization.
  • Balanced the dataset by downsampling overrepresented classes and augmenting underrepresented ones for a more robust training distribution.
  • Applied transfer learning on Inception V3, replacing the final layer with a custom classification head, then fine-tuned using cross-entropy loss and the Adam optimizer.
